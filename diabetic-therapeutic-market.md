<p><p>The diabetic therapeutic market comprises various drug classes used to manage blood sugar levels. Sulphonylureas stimulate insulin secretion, while biguanides, like metformin, enhance insulin sensitivity and reduce hepatic glucose production. Meglitinides also promote insulin release but act quickly. Thiazolidinediones improve insulin sensitivity in muscle and fat tissues. Alpha-glucosidase inhibitors delay carbohydrate absorption in the intestines. DPP-4 inhibitors increase incretin levels, enhancing insulin secretion. SGLT-2 inhibitors lower blood glucose by promoting urinary glucose excretion, offering additional benefits like weight loss.</p></p>
